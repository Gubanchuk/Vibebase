  Clear five-point summary of structural differences:
  (1) No articles in Russian,
  (2) Aspect system vs. tense system,
  (3) Flexible word order thanks to case endings (English relies on position),
  (4) "To be" often dropped in Russian present tense,
  (5) Motion verbs encode direction/path, not just movement.
  Each difference is the root of a specific error family in the Russian
  speaker's English.

# How 4/3/2 works

### Setup
- One topic (e.g. «describe your morning routine», «talk about last
  weekend»)
- Same speaker, three different listeners (partners rotate)
- Time constraints: 4 min → 3 min → 2 min

### Sequence
1. **4 min** — first attempt, rambling OK, figure out what to say
2. **3 min** — same topic, already knows structure, compresses
3. **2 min** — same topic, fluent delivery, automatic

### Effect
- Fluency (WPM) increases ~30% from attempt 1 to attempt 3
- Hesitations reduce significantly
- Grammar accuracy **slightly decreases** (speed > accuracy trade-off)
- Content density increases

# Why it works

### Automation
Each retelling lets working memory offload structure → attention goes
to delivery (pacing, intonation).

### Procedural repetition (see separate vault item)
Same topic, different structure (rehearsing under pressure) >
same topic, same structure.

### Reduced planning overhead
Third time: planning what to say takes 0% cognitive budget. 100% goes
to speaking.

# AI-tutor adaptation

- **Mode 1 (chat)** v2: «let's do a 4/3/2 on topic X». User types
  4-min answer, AI gives 3-min follow-up prompt, then 2-min.
- **Voice mode** (v3): real audio-timed 4/3/2 with user speaking.
  AI-partner играет roles (colleague, interviewer, friend).

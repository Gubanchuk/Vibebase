# Why it's in the vault

Every design decision for Mode 1 (Native chat) should be traceable to one of
Krashen's three conditions. Specifically: do NOT correct aggressively during
conversation (anxiety ↑, acquisition ↓); do NOT force output before the
learner's ready; DO supply rich, level-appropriate input with scaffolding.

Mode 5 (Grammar explainer) violates pure Krashen doctrine — it's explicit
instruction — but pedagogically valuable for adults who want the meta-rule.
We split modes precisely so a user can choose acquisition (Mode 1) vs.
learning (Mode 5).

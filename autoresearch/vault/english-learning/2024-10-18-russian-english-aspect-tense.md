tldr: |
  Russian has 2 aspects (perfective/imperfective), English has ~12 tense
  forms. The mapping is many-to-many: Russian imperfective can map to
  English simple OR continuous; perfective often maps to simple past
  OR present perfect depending on time reference. This is why Russian
  speakers over-use Simple and under-use Perfect.

# Tutor instruction

When the user produces a simple-past sentence that should be present-perfect
(e.g., "I lived here since 2020"), don't just correct — explain via the
aspect bridge: "In Russian you'd use imperfective здесь to show ongoing;
English uses Present Perfect Continuous for that exact meaning."

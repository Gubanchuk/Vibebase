# Curated english-learning — top 6

Selection as of 2026-04-22.

## The 6

1. **[Krashen i+1 canonical](../../english-learning/2025-09-10-krashen-input-hypothesis-i-plus-1.md)** — the theoretical spine of every calibrated-content decision in the tutor. Bake the 90/10 rule into system prompts.

2. **[LLM input + oral proficiency study](../../english-learning/2025-11-12-frontiers-llm-input-oral-proficiency.md)** — empirical confirmation: the LLM must get the level right within ~5%, or the i+1 effect attenuates. Quantitative budget for calibration accuracy.

3. **[Russian L1 — articles + tenses](../../english-learning/2025-10-15-russian-english-article-omission.md)** — high-frequency errors our mistake-extractor must catch. Drives the heuristic ruleset for Mode 1 Native chat.

4. **[Russian L1 — preposition transfer](../../english-learning/2025-09-01-russian-english-prepositions.md)** — paired with #3; these two together cover the bulk of B1-level error traffic.

5. **[Oxford Placement Test methodology](../../english-learning/2025-08-22-oxford-placement-adaptive.md)** — reference point for v1.1 adaptive placement; benchmark our flow against this.

6. **[Prompt engineering 2025 — applied to tutoring](../../english-learning/2025-08-05-codesignal-prompt-engineering-2025.md)** — directly translates into our 6 mode system prompts. The "Claude over-explains unless bounded" note alone saves real tokens.

## Why not these

- *Aspect-vs-tense teaching order* (strong) — very useful, but it lives more naturally inside a Skills curriculum spec than as a tutor-prompt input.
- *Adaptive placement length research* (strong) — folded into the understanding behind #5, not needed separately in curated.
- *CEFR-J writing assessment* — future v1.1 feature; not MVP-relevant.
